What is IAM?

Identity and Access Management (IAM) is the discipline of ensuring the right people have the right access to the right resources at the right time for the right reasons.

Core IAM Components

ComponentPurposeTechnologies
Directory Services Store and manage identities Active Directory, Entra ID, LDAP
Authentication Verify user identity Kerberos, SAML, OIDC, MFA
Authorization Control what users can do RBAC, ABAC, Conditional Access
SSO/Federation Single login for multiple apps Okta, Entra ID, Ping Identity
Identity Governance Manage access lifecycle SailPoint, Saviynt, One Identity
Privileged Access Secure admin/root access CyberArk, BeyondTrust, Delinea

Day in the Life: IAM Engineer by Level

  • Morning: Process access requests queue (ServiceNow tickets)
  • 10 AM: Reset passwords, unlock accounts, resolve MFA issues
  • Afternoon: Provision new hire accounts from HR feed
  • 3 PM: Disable accounts for terminated employees
  • End of day: Document completed tickets, escalate complex issues

Tools: AD Users & Computers, Azure Portal, Okta Admin, ServiceNow

  • Morning: Review overnight sync errors, troubleshoot failed provisioning
  • 10 AM: Configure new application SSO integration (SAML/OIDC)
  • Afternoon: Write PowerShell automation for user onboarding
  • 3 PM: Participate in access certification review
  • End of day: Update runbooks, mentor junior team members

Tools: PowerShell, Graph API, SAML tracer, SailPoint

  • Morning: Architecture review for new Zero Trust initiative
  • 10 AM: Lead IAM security review with InfoSec team
  • Afternoon: Design Conditional Access policies for compliance
  • 3 PM: Vendor evaluation for PAM solution replacement
  • End of day: Strategic planning for cloud identity migration

Focus: Architecture, strategy, cross-team leadership, complex troubleshooting
