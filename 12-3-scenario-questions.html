Scenario 1: Compromised Admin Account

üìã Scenario

"You receive an alert that a Global Administrator account has signed in from an unusual location. The user claims they didn't sign in. What do you do?"

‚úÖ Solution Approach

Immediate (First 15 minutes):

  • Revoke all refresh tokens: Revoke-MgUserSignInSession
  • Force password reset
  • Disable the account temporarily
  • Check for MFA bypass (was MFA used?)

Investigation:

  • Review sign-in logs for suspicious activity
  • Check audit logs for changes made during compromise window
  • Look for new app registrations, role assignments, conditional access changes
  • Check for mailbox rules or forwarding (Exchange)

Remediation:

  • Re-register MFA methods (attacker may have added their own)
  • Review and revert unauthorized changes
  • Implement stricter Conditional Access (device compliance, location)
  • Enable PIM for Global Admin (no standing access)

Scenario 2: Orphaned Accounts

üìã Scenario

"An audit reveals 500 active accounts for employees who left the company over the past year. How do you address this and prevent it?"

‚úÖ Solution Approach

Immediate Cleanup:

  • Cross-reference AD/Entra with HR system (employee status)
  • Disable accounts for confirmed terminations
  • Move to "Disabled Users" OU with 30-day deletion policy
  • Revoke all sessions and tokens immediately

Prevention (Root Cause Fix):

  • Implement HR-driven provisioning (Workday/SAP ‚Üí Entra)
  • Use Lifecycle Workflows for automated offboarding
  • Set up quarterly access certifications
  • Create "stale account" reports (no sign-in > 90 days)

Scenario 3: SSO Outage

üìã Scenario

"Users report they can't access any applications. Your IdP (Okta/Entra) appears to be having issues. How do you handle this?"

‚úÖ Solution Approach

Immediate:

  • Check IdP status page (status.okta.com, status.azure.com)
  • Verify it's not a local network/DNS issue
  • Communicate to users that issue is known

Business Continuity:

  • Use break-glass accounts for critical systems
  • Enable local authentication for critical apps (if available)
  • Activate emergency IdP (if HA federation configured)

Prevention:

  • Ensure break-glass accounts exist and are tested monthly
  • Consider multi-IdP federation for critical apps
  • Document and test IdP failure runbook

Scenario 4: MFA Fatigue Attack

üìã Scenario

"A user reports receiving many MFA push notifications they didn't request. They accidentally approved one. What happened and how do you respond?"

‚úÖ Solution Approach

What Happened: MFA fatigue/bombing attack. Attacker has the password and spammed push notifications hoping user would approve.

Immediate:

  • Revoke all sessions for the user
  • Force password reset
  • Review activity since the approved push
  • Check for persistence (new MFA methods, app passwords)

Prevention:

  • Enable number matching (user must enter code from screen)
  • Show additional context (location, app) in push
  • Limit push attempts before lockout
  • Consider phishing-resistant MFA (FIDO2)
  • User training: Never approve unexpected MFA prompts

Scenario 5: Merger/Acquisition

üìã Scenario

"Your company is acquiring another company with 5,000 employees. They use a different IdP. How do you approach identity integration?"

‚úÖ Solution Approach

Day 1 (Quick Wins):

  • Set up B2B federation (guest access) for immediate collaboration
  • Federate their IdP to your apps (or vice versa)
  • No migration needed yet, just trust

Assessment Phase:

  • Inventory both environments (apps, users, groups, policies)
  • Identify overlapping applications
  • Determine target state (consolidate to one IdP?)
  • Assess licensing implications

Migration (if consolidating):

  • Staged migration by department/region
  • Parallel running period
  • User communication and training
  • Coexistence for legacy apps
